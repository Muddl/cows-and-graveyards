### Consensus Strengths

1. **Visual quality and correctness are strong** (avg 3.6 / 3.7) — the pastoral 3D environment, cow model, custom shaders, and particle effects are above prototype quality. Every agent acknowledged visual charm.
2. **Performance perception is solid** (avg 3.9) — the Mobile renderer + Jolt Physics stack is well-matched to scene complexity. No agent reported stuttering or performance concerns.
3. **Core mechanic is intuitive** — tap-to-count maps to the real car game. Once in-game, the physical interaction works.
4. **72pt score font** is universally appreciated for readability.
5. **Save slot system** is thoughtful for the shared-device, multi-leg road trip context.

### Consensus Issues

1. **Zero audio** — flagged by ALL 7 agents as the #1 or #2 issue. The silence makes every interaction feel dead, broken, or unconfirmed. This is the single most impactful missing feature.
2. **No onboarding/tutorial** — flagged by 6 agents (Casey, Milo, Avery, Jordan, Reese, Morgan). First-time players cannot understand the game without prior knowledge of the road trip tradition.
3. **No tap feedback beyond score increment** — flagged by 6 agents. Missing haptics, tap ripple, score animation, and audio confirmation make taps feel unregistered.
4. **Graveyard button is dangerous and unexplained** — flagged by 4 agents (Milo, Jordan, Dakota, Morgan). Silently zeroing a score with no confirmation feels like a bug, not a mechanic.
5. **Startup friction / no quick-resume** — flagged by 3 agents (Milo, Reese, Morgan). Menu → slot selection → game is too many taps; repeat users need a "continue last trip" shortcut.
6. **Score labels ("L: X" / "R: X") are cryptic** — flagged by 4 agents. No player names, no context, unclear if solo or multiplayer.
